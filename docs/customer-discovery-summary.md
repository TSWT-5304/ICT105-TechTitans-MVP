# Customer Discovery Summary

## Project Title
PeerTutor – Student-to-Student Tutoring Marketplace

## Target Users Studied
- Rangsit university students who seek academic help.
- Rangsit university students who want to become tutors and earn extra money.

## Number of Responses / Interviews
15 students and 7 tutors responses collected.

## Main Repeated Problem
Students struggle to find trustworthy, affordable, and available tutors quickly when they need verified academic help for exams, assignments, projects, and coursework.

Tutors also face challenges in reaching students, managing communication, scheduling sessions, and demonstrating their qualifications and credibility.

## Main Current Workarounds
Students currently find help through:
- Friends (4 responses)
- Facebook groups (3 responses)
- Senior students (4 responses)
- Line groups (2 responses)
- Private tutors (2 responses)

Tutors currently find students through:
- Friends and Classmates (2 responses)
- Seniors (1 responses)
- Facebook groups (2 responses)
- Line groups (1 responses)
- Lecturer recommendation (1 responses)

## Strongest Evidence Found
The strongest evidence is that nearly all respondents rely on informal channels such as friends, seniors, Facebook groups, and Line groups instead of a dedicated tutoring platform.

Common student frustrations included:
- Slow response times.
- Difficulty trusting tutors.
- Scheduling conflicts.
- Limited tutor availability.
- Lack of tutor ratings and reviews.
- High tutoring costs.

Common tutor frustrations included:
- Difficulty scheduling sessions.
- Difficulty reaching more students.
- Excessive communication time.
- Lack of a rating or reputation system.
- Students being unable to see tutor qualifications.

Many students stated that failure to find a tutor could lead to lower grades, missed deadlines, incomplete projects, or poor understanding of course material.
