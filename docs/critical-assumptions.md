# Critical Assumptions

| Assumption ID | Category | Assumption | Related Requirement/User Story | Risk Level | Current Evidence | How to Test |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A-01 | User Problem | Students experience difficulty finding trustworthy peer tutors because tutor information is scattered across multiple platforms. | FR-01, FR-05 / US-01, US-02 | High | Lab 03 survey and customer interviews | Observe whether students successfully use Tutor Search instead of relying on Facebook or LINE groups. |
| A-02 | Value Proposition | Students prefer a centralized tutoring platform over existing communication channels. | FR-01, FR-06 / US-01, US-02 | High | Customer Discovery findings | Ask users whether they would use PeerTutor instead of current methods after completing the prototype tasks. |
| A-03 | Usability | Students can search, compare tutors, and submit tutoring requests without guidance. | FR-06, FR-13 / US-02, US-04 | High | Wireframe review and prototype design | Conduct task-based usability testing and record task completion success. |
| A-04 | Usability | Tutors can complete the registration and application process without confusion. | FR-03, FR-13 / US-09 | Medium | Prototype workflow | Observe tutors completing the registration workflow and collect usability feedback. |
| A-05 | Business Logic | Administrator approval increases trust in tutor quality. | FR-11 / US-13, US-14 | Medium | Business Model Canvas | Interview users about whether verified tutors increase their confidence in choosing tutors. |
| A-06 | Technical Feasibility | HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Google Sheets are sufficient to demonstrate the MVP. | Technical Architecture | Medium | Lab 06 Technical Architecture | Verify that all prototype workflows operate correctly during demonstrations. |
| A-07 | Data Handling | Google Sheets can reliably store prototype records such as tutor applications, tutoring requests, and reviews. | FR-04, FR-18 | Medium | Lab 06 Data Structure | Submit multiple records and verify that all data is stored correctly. |
| A-08 | Business Logic | Simulated online payment is sufficient for evaluating the tutoring workflow without a real payment gateway. | FR-07, FR-12 / US-06, US-15 | Medium | MVP scope | Observe whether users understand the payment process during prototype testing. |
| A-09 | Usability | Students understand the tutoring request status labels (Pending, Approved, Rejected, Completed, Cancelled). | FR-10 / US-05 | Medium | Wireframe design | Ask participants to explain each request status during usability testing. |
| A-10 | Usability | Students trust tutor ratings and reviews when selecting tutors. | FR-08, FR-14 / US-03, US-08 | Medium | Customer Discovery | Ask participants whether ratings influence their tutor selection decisions. |
| A-11 | Technical Feasibility | Dashboard summaries and analytics are understandable and useful for administrators. | FR-15 / US-16 | Low | Dashboard wireframes | Observe administrators completing monitoring tasks using the dashboard. |
