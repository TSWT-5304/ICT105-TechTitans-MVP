# Problem Notes

## Selected Case
PeerTutor – Student-to-Student Tutoring Marketplace

## Target Respondents

| Respondent Type | Why This Respondent Matters | Planned Number | Actual Number |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| Students seeking academic help | Experience difficulties finding suitable tutors | 15 | 15 |
| Student tutors | Experience difficulties promoting tutoring services and managing requests | 7 | 7 |

## Observed / Reported Problem

University students often struggle to find suitable tutors for specific subjects. Most respondents rely on Facebook groups, Line groups, friends, seniors, or private tutors. These methods provide limited information about tutor quality, availability, experience, and reliability. Student tutors also face challenges promoting their services and reaching potential learners.

| Consequence | Example | Evidence Strength |
|---|---|---|
| Poor academic performance | Lower grades, fail assessments, poor understanding of subjects | Strong |
| Missed deadlines | Assignments and projects not completed on time | Strong |
| Reduced tutoring opportunities | Tutors struggle to find students and earn income | Moderate |
| Time wasted searching | Students spend time checking multiple channels | Moderate |

## Notes for MVP Direction

The MVP should focus on solving the most frequently reported problems:

- Create a centralized tutor listing page.
- Allow students to search tutors by subject.
- Provide tutor profiles with experience and subject expertise.
- Include availability information.
- Implement a basic tutoring request form.
- Add ratings and reviews to increase trust.
- Provide tutor registration to improve tutor visibility.

The MVP should validate whether a university-specific tutoring platform can simplify tutor discovery and improve communication between students and tutors.
