# Lab 05 Practice Case Brief 
## PeerTutor — Student-to-Student Tutoring Marketplace

## Case Context
Rangsit university international students often struggle to find trustworthy peer tutors when preparing for examinations, completing assignments, or learning difficult subjects. Most students currently rely on Facebook groups, Line groups, recommendations from friends, or senior students to find tutors. These methods are scattered, time-consuming, and make it difficult to compare tutors' qualifications, availability, pricing, and reviews before making a decision.
At the same time, student tutors also face difficulties promoting their tutoring services because there is no centralized platform where they can create professional tutor profiles and connect with students who need academic support.
## Target Users

# Primary user
- Rangsit University students who need academic tutoring support 
- Student tutors who want to offer tutoring services and promote themselves. 

# Secondary user
- Platform admin who approves tutors, manages accounts, and monitors platform activity. 

## Product Direction
The proposed product is a web-based student-to-student tutoring marketplace that allows students to search tutors, view tutor profiles, submit tutoring requests, track request status, and leave ratings and reviews. Student tutors can submit a tutor application, create a tutor profile after admin approval, receive tutoring requests, manage tutoring sessions, and view student ratings and reviews.

## Lab 05 Practice Purpose
This project applies the product design method from Lab 04 to develop the PeerTutor MVP by defining the product concept, designing wireframes, and preparing the prototype for implementation. 
#Note on Method
This lab followed the practice-then-apply approach outlined above: we first reviewed the "Smart Campus Lost-and-Found System" case to understand how to derive product concept, feature-requirement mapping, and screen inventory from a set of requirements. We then applied the same method to our own group case, PeerTutor – Student-to-Student Tutoring Marketplace, using the persona, requirements, and user stories developed in Lab 04.
