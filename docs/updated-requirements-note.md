# Updated Requirements Note 
## Purpose
This file records requirement changes identified during the Lab 09 legal, ethical, privacy, intellectual property, and security review.

The review did not require a major change to the PeerTutor MVP direction. However, several existing requirements should be clarified to improve privacy, transparency, access control, data security, and legal compliance before further implementation.

| Requirement ID | Original Requirement | Proposed Update | Reason for Change | Supporting Evidence | GitHub Issue/Commit | Approved by the Team? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **FR-03** | The system shall provide forms for tutoring requests, tutor registration, and other required user input. | All forms shall collect only necessary information, clearly identify required and optional fields, and validate incomplete or invalid input before submission. | The privacy and security reviews found a risk of unnecessary data collection and invalid form submissions. | Privacy and Data Protection Review; Basic Security Risk Check R-01 | Issue: Apply data minimization and form validation | Yes |
| **FR-07** | The system shall allow students to complete an online payment process. | For the MVP, the payment function shall be clearly labelled as a simulation. It shall not collect or store real bank accounts, card, password, or financial authentication information. | Users must not misunderstand the prototype payment process as a real financial service. Real financial information would also create unnecessary privacy and security risks. | Legal and Ethical Checklist; Risk Register R-02; Test User Notes T-04 | Issue: Clarify simulated payment workflow | Yes |
| **FR-08** | Student Tutors shall be able to create and manage tutor profiles. | Tutor profile information shall be divided into public profile information and private verification information. Tutors shall be able to preview the public profile before publishing changes. | Tutors requested a profile preview, and the privacy review found that verification information should not be publicly displayed. | Lab 08 Test User Notes TU-07; Privacy Review | Issue: Add profile preview and privacy controls | Yes |
| **FR-09** | Users shall be able to view payment history. | Payment history shall display only necessary transaction information, such as reference number, amount, date, and status. It shall not display confidential payment credentials. | Payment records must follow data-minimization and privacy principles. | Privacy and Data Protection Review; Risk Register R-01 | Issue: Minimize payment-history data | Yes |
| **FR-10** | The system shall display tutoring requests and payment statuses. | Status updates shall use clear labels and progress indicators. Only authorized users shall be able to modify a request, payment, application, or transfer status. | Lab 08 testing identified confusion with status labels, while the security review identified uncontrolled record editing as a risk. | Analytics Insights; Test User Notes; Basic Security Risk Check | Issue: Improve status indicators and update controls | Yes |
| **FR-11** | Administrators shall manage tutor approvals, users, and platform activities. | Administrative functions shall be restricted to authenticated Administrator accounts and separated from Student and Tutor functions. Important administrative changes should be recorded in an activity log in future implementation. | The Lab 09 review identified unauthorized access to administrator functions as a security risk. | Legal and Ethical Checklist; Basic Security Risk Check R-04 | Issue: Strengthen admin access control | Yes |
| **FR-12** | Administrators shall verify payments and manage tutor earnings. | Payment verification, platform commission, tutor earnings, and earnings transfers shall be displayed as separate administrative functions. Only authorized administrators may approve or change these records. | Administrators requested clearer workflow separation, and payment-related records require stronger control. | Test User Notes T-10; Analytics Insights; Risk Register R-04 | Issue: Separate payment and earnings controls | Yes |
| **FR-13** | The system shall provide clear form instructions, validation, and confirmation messages. | Submission forms shall explain why personal information is needed and show what will happen after submission. Consent or acknowledgement shall be included where appropriate. | Users must understand how their information is used and what follows after submitting a request, application, payment, or review. | Legal and Ethical Checklist; Privacy Rule for Prototype | Issue: Add submission and privacy explanations | Yes |
| **FR-15** | Dashboards shall display role-specific information and analytics. | Dashboards shall show only information required by the logged-in role. Analytics shall use aggregated or non-identifying information whenever individual identity is unnecessary. | Analytics and dashboards could expose private information if the same data is shown to every role. | Privacy Review; Basic Security Risk Check; Risk Register R-01 | Issue: Apply role-based dashboard visibility | Yes |
| **FR-16** | The system shall maintain a consistent and understandable user interface. | The prototype shall include accessible links to a Privacy Notice, Terms of Use, prototype limitation statement, and third-party asset credits where required. | Lab 09 identified missing legal notices and possible third-party attribution requirements. | Legal and Ethical Checklist; IP Register; Risk Register R-03 and R-05 | Issue: Add legal notices and asset credits | Yes |
| **FR-17** | The prototype shall operate across suitable desktop and mobile screen sizes. | Privacy notices, consent information, status messages, and security warnings shall remain visible and readable on both desktop and mobile layouts. | Important legal and privacy information must not be hidden or unreadable on smaller screens. | Legal and Ethical Checklist; Responsive UI Review | Issue: Review legal content responsiveness | Yes |
| **FR-19** | System features and screens shall remain traceable to approved requirements. | Every legal, privacy, ethical, IP, or security-related requirement change shall be linked to a GitHub issue or commit and approved by the team before implementation. | Requirement changes must not be made silently and must remain traceable across project documents. | Lab 09 Updated Requirements Rule; Risk Register | Issue: Maintain Lab 09 requirement traceability | Yes |

## Team Decision
The team approved these updates as **clarifications and minor revisions** to the existing MVP requirements. The updates do not change the central PeerTutor solution or its main Student, Student Tutor, and Administrator workflows.

The requirements should be updated in [`system-requirements.md`](https://github.com/TSWT-5304/ICT105-TechTitans-MVP/blob/d52fc5bd4ed2e9d2adc48de3062e6ce602daf7e6/docs/system-requirements.md) only after the related GitHub issues are created and the team confirms the final wording.

## Rule
Do not silently change system requirements. Every change must be justified, documented, approved by the team, and connected to supporting evidence and a GitHub issue or commit.
